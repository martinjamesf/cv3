Case Study: Corporate Communications Research and Design

Modernizing enterprise communications for a remote-first workforce

Challenge

With 83% of American Family Insurance’s workforce already remote, internal communications were fragmented across tools and channels. The challenge was to create a unified experience that met the diverse needs of employees, leaders, and advisors—without overwhelming any one group.

Workshop 1: Discovery

We interviewed stakeholders and frontline employees to uncover key pain points:

  • Leaders needed faster access to strategic and timely updates.
  • Employees wanted better search, personalization, and tool discovery.
  • Sales teams sought quick access to field support and policy news.

Workshop 2: Experimentation

We tested rapid prototypes to validate personalization preferences, card-based content models, and navigation simplification. We ensured stakeholder alignment by addressing regulatory compliance and internal editorial guidelines.

Design Outcome

We designed a unified homepage for internal users with personalized modules, streamlined navigation, and quick access to critical tools and content. The interface reflected both business priorities and employee needs.
